The Titanic, a British luxury liner, was one of the largest and most luxurious ships in the world when it embarked on its maiden voyage. Registered at 46,328 tons, the ship had a displacement of over 52,000 tons when fully loaded. The Titanic was approximately 882.5 feet (269 meters) long and about 92.5 feet (28.2 meters) wide. On April 14-15, 1912, during its maiden voyage from Southampton, England to New York City, the Titanic sank, resulting in the deaths of approximately 1,500 passengers and crew members, making it one of the most famous tragedies in modern history.
